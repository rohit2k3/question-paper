Define the expression for form factor and peak factor

Form factor is defined as the ratio of the RMS value of the waveform to its average value over one complete cycle. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

Form factor = RMS value of waveform / Average value of waveform over one cycle

Peak factor is defined as the ratio of the peak value of the waveform to its RMS value. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

Peak factor = Peak value of waveform / RMS value of waveform

Define electromotive force

Electromotive force is defined as the electric potential produced by either an electrochemical cell or by changing the magnetic field.

Define power factor

Power factor is a measure of how efficiently electrical power is being used in an AC (alternating current) electrical system. It is defined as the ratio of the real power (measured in watts) to the apparent power (measured in volt-amperes). In other words, power factor is a measure of how much of the total electrical power being supplied to a system is actually being used to do useful work, and how much is being wasted.

Write the statement of superposition theorem

In any linear and bilateral network or circuit having multiple independent sources, the response of an element will be equal to the algebraic sum of the responses of that element by considering one source at a time

What is the function of commutator in DC generator

Its function is to convert the alternating current (AC) produced by the generator into direct current (DC), which can be used to power electrical devices.

What is KVA rating of a transformer

The KVA rating of a transformer refers to the maximum apparent power (measured in kilovolt-amperes or KVA) that the transformer can handle or deliver at a given voltage and frequency. It is a measure of the transformer's capacity to handle electrical power.

Define a slip of an induction motor

A slip in an induction motor refers to the difference between the synchronous speed of the rotating magnetic field in the stator and the actual speed of the rotor.
Slip is expressed as a percentage of the synchronous speed and is calculated using the following formula:

Slip = (Synchronous speed - Actual speed)/Synchronous speed x 100%

What is called synchronous speed in AC machines

Synchronous speed in AC machines refers to the speed at which the rotating magnetic field generated by the stator of the machine rotates. This speed is determined by the frequency of the AC power supply and the number of poles in the machine. Specifically, the synchronous speed of an AC machine can be calculated using the following formula:.

Synchronous speed (in revolutions per minute) = (120 x frequency) / number of poles

Define ideal voltage and current source

An ideal current source is by definition a two-terminal element with the property that the current flowing through the device is specified at every instant in time.

By definition, an ideal voltage source is a two-terminal element with the property that the voltage across the terminals is specified at every instant in time.

Define active and passive elements

An active component is an electronic component which supplies energy to a circuit. Active elements have the ability to electrically control electron flow (i.e. the flow of charge). All electronic circuits must contain at least one active component.

An passive component is an electronic component which can only receive energy, which it can either dissipate, absorb or store it in an electric field or a magnetic field. Passive elements do not need any form of electrical power to operate.

Classify the types of losses in transformer

We know that reactive loads such as inductors and capacitors dissipate zero power, yet the fact that they drop voltage and draw current gives the deceptive impression that they actually do dissipate power. This “phantom power” is called reactive power.

The actual amount of power being used, or dissipated, in a circuit is called true power.

The combination of reactive power and true power is called apparent power.

What do you mean by the term speed regulation

The speed regulation of a motor is defined as the change in the speed from full-load to no-load and is expressed as a percentage of the full-load speed

Write down the applications of synchronous motor

The applications of synchronous motor are

1. Power Factor Correction: Synchronous motors can be used to improve the power factor of electrical systems by generating reactive power.

2. Generators: Synchronous motors can be operated as generators to produce electrical power.

3. Electric Vehicles: Synchronous motors are used in electric vehicles because of their high efficiency, power density, and low maintenance requirements.

Write down the applications of single phase induction motor

The applications of single phase induction motor are

1. Household Appliances: Single phase induction motors are used in household appliances such as washing machines, refrigerators, air conditioners, and fans.

2. Office Equipment: They are also used in office equipment such as printers, scanners, and photocopiers.

3. Water Pumps: Single phase induction motors are widely used in water pumps for residential, commercial, and agricultural purposes.
